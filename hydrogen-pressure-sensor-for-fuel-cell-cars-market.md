<p><p>Hydrogen pressure sensors for fuel cell cars come in several types, each utilizing different detection methods. Catalytic sensors rely on chemical reactions to measure hydrogen levels. Electrochemical sensors use electrochemical reactions to detect gas concentration. Metal oxide sensors function by monitoring changes in electrical resistance in the presence of hydrogen. Other types may include optical or thermal sensors, offering alternative detection mechanisms. Each type plays a critical role in ensuring safety and efficiency in fuel cell vehicle operations.</p></p>
