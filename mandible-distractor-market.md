<p><p>The mandible distractor market is categorized into two primary types: multi-vector distraction and bi-directional distraction. Multi-vector distraction devices allow movement in multiple directions, enabling complex jaw repositioning, which is beneficial for treating severe deformities. In contrast, bi-directional distraction focuses on two opposing directions, facilitating more straightforward adjustments in jaw lengthening or repositioning. Both types cater to diverse patient needs in maxillofacial surgery, enhancing treatment outcomes and improving functional and aesthetic results.</p></p>
