<p><p>Point-to-point bike sharing includes two main market types: docked bikes and dockless bikes. Docked bike systems require users to rent bikes from designated stations and return them to the same or another station, promoting organization and easier management. Conversely, dockless bike systems offer greater flexibility, allowing users to pick up and drop off bikes anywhere within a designated area, enhancing accessibility. Both models aim to encourage eco-friendly transportation, reduce traffic congestion, and provide convenient mobility options for urban dwellers.</p></p>
