<p><p>Supercritical Fluid Chromatography (SFC) is a powerful separation technique that utilizes supercritical fluids, typically carbon dioxide, as the mobile phase for analyzing and separating compounds. This method combines the high diffusion coefficients of gases with the high density of liquids, allowing for efficient sample separation with reduced solvent usage. SFC is particularly advantageous in pharmaceuticals, environmental analysis, and food safety due to its fast analysis times and ability to handle heat-sensitive materials.</p><p>The SFC market is experiencing significant growth, projected to increase at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period. Recent trends indicate a focus on hybrid techniques, integrating SFC with mass spectrometry for improved analytical capabilities.
