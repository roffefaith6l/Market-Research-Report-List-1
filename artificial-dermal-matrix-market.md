<p><p>The Artificial Dermal Matrix market comprises two key types based on the source: Animal-derived and Human-derived matrices. Animal sources typically utilize materials such as porcine or bovine collagen, which are known for their biocompatibility and structural support in wound healing. In contrast, human-derived matrices are made from cadaveric tissues, offering a more compatible solution for patients due to their similarity to human biology, thus promoting better integration and reducing rejection risks. Both types cater to diverse clinical needs in regenerative medicine.</p></p>
